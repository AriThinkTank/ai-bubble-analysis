Sustainability Condition (NPV Framework)
From documents — investment is sustainable only if: NPV = Σ(CFt / (1+r)t) − Cumulative Capex ≥ 0 At r = 12% discount rate, 15-yr depreciation, this requires annual cash flows averaging ~15–20% of cumulative capex. Required avg. annual CF ≥ $560B × 0.175 = ~$98B/yr Current AI revenue trajectory: ~$35B (2025) → implies 3× growth needed just to break sustainability threshold. Break-even expected 2028–2030.
The Circular Investment Loop — System Instability

Nvidia invests $100B in OpenAI → OpenAI buys Nvidia chips → Nvidia revenues rise → Nvidia market cap rises → more capacity to invest. Goldman Sachs estimates 15% of Nvidia's 2025 sales come from such circular arrangements.

R = α·I,   I = β·V,   V = γ·R Substituting: R(1 − αβγ) = 0 System stable only if αβγ < 1. As circular exposure grows, αβγ → 1 and the system approaches criticality.

Global Cascade — Dependency Map
How a bubble correction propagates through the global economy — from US financial markets through to emerging economies.
Phase 1: Months 0–6

Trigger event (revenue miss, capability plateau, or macro shock). Foundation lab valuation collapse. Nvidia stock drops 30–40%. US tech NASDAQ correction begins. VC funding for AI freezes. Circular investment loop begins to unravel.

Phase 2: Months 6–18

Hyperscaler capex cuts announced. Enterprise AI budgets frozen. Startup ecosystem mass layoffs. TSMC chip demand signal drops. Global equity contagion: −10 to −25% in tech-heavy indices. India IT sector feels demand compression.

Phase 3: Months 18–36+

Regulatory response (panic rules or safety dilution). "Dark compute" overcapacity → inference cost collapse. Survivors emerge stronger. India's application-layer and sovereign AI programs partially recover. Long-term: cheap infrastructure enables next generation.

Leverage Points & Complexity Analysis
Where small changes produce disproportionate system outcomes — using Meadows' leverage point framework applied to the AI investment system.
1
Revenue Realisation Threshold (~$300–500B by 2028)
The single most critical leverage point. If enterprise AI revenue fails to reach $300B annually within 3 years, capital markets will reassess — triggering cascading devaluation. Every quarter of revenue data is a system signal. Current trajectory: $37B (2025) needing 8–13× growth in 3 years.
2
Circular Financing Stability (αβγ < 1)
If any node in the Nvidia → OpenAI → Cloud loop faces financial stress, the feedback reverses. Goldman Sachs: 15% of Nvidia revenue is circular. A single OpenAI funding round failure could destabilise the entire loop. Monitoring: OpenAI's next fundraise and revenue update.
3
Capability Trajectory — Scaling Returns
If marginal value of larger models stops exceeding marginal cost, the investment thesis collapses. Gary Marcus and others note disappointing returns to scale at frontier. Conversely, a reasoning breakthrough resets the entire analysis. This is the highest-uncertainty leverage point.
4
Interest Rate Environment
Higher rates compress DCF valuations of long-dated AI revenue streams. At 5% discount rate: NPV of $2T in 2030 revenue = $1.56T. At 8%: $1.26T. A 3-point rate rise reduces AI asset values ~20% independently of operational performance.
5
Sovereign / Geopolitical Floor
US-China AI competition creates a political floor on investment that doesn't exist in normal bubble dynamics. Government backing (CHIPS Act, IndiaAI Mission, EU AI Act investment) prevents full capital withdrawal. This is the key reason AI differs from crypto — state actors underwrite a base level of investment.
6
Enterprise Adoption Gap Closure (25% shadow AI → formal)
40% of knowledge workers use AI personally; only 15% have formal enterprise deployment. Closing this 25-point gap from bottom-up adoption could generate $100–200B in new enterprise revenue rapidly, validating investments and averting correction. The "Slack-ification" of AI is the soft-landing mechanism.

Synthesis: Is This a Bubble?

The documents' core thesis — and this analysis concurs — is that AI is simultaneously a bubble and a transformative technology. These are not mutually exclusive. The dotcom era showed that bubbles can finance infrastructure that outlasts the correction. The critical distinction from crypto: AI has demonstrated measurable utility (14% productivity gains, 78% enterprise adoption) while crypto's utility-speculation ratio never broke 0.001.

The mathematical structure reveals a system under stress: $560B invested yielding $35B revenue (6¢/$1), OpenAI needing 72% CAGR, and circular financing loops approaching αβγ = 1. Yet the game-theoretic analysis shows rational actors cannot stop investing. The most likely outcome is Scenario A (35%) — a productive bubble correction, not unlike dark fiber — leaving "dark compute" that enables the next wave of applications. For India, the application-layer focus and sovereign programs provide partial insulation, but the IT sector's North American dependence remains the primary vulnerability, with a 6–12 month lag before any US correction reaches Indian shores.
